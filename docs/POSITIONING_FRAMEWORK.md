# Positioning Framework

## 1. Purpose

The positioning engine structures employee judgment. It does not convert a questionnaire directly into a single answer. Every score must cite evidence, confidence, and execution constraints.

## 2. Eight diagnosis modules

| Module | Required dimensions | Key output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Creator assets | identity, experience, expertise, personality, speaking, trust, camera presence, controversy tolerance | Amplifiable strengths and hard limits |
| Pet assets | appearance, personality, behavior, stories, camera/content/live fit, story potential | Pet role card |
| Human-pet relationship | model, emotional value, recurring conflict/story, identification, change | Relationship narrative |
| Audience | core, extended, excluded, ownership stage, lifestyle, needs, scenarios, barriers, spending | Prioritized audience segments |
| Content architecture | traffic, trust, persona, conversion, ratios, formats, difficulty | Sustainable content system |
| Platform adaptation | Douyin, Xiaohongshu, WeChat Channels, Taobao content, live, private domain | Goal and format by platform |
| Commercial potential | sponsorship, short video, live, own brand, affiliate, consulting, courses, community, local/offline | Phased monetization path |
| Execution | time, filming, editing, location, pet cooperation, inventory, products, team, service, budget | Ideal, executable, and minimum-start versions |

## 3. Evidence scale

Each dimension scores 0-100:

- `0-39`: insufficient or conflicting evidence;
- `40-59`: possible with meaningful constraints;
- `60-79`: usable and supported;
- `80-100`: distinctive, repeatable, and evidenced.

A high market-opportunity score cannot erase a low execution score. The UI must show both.

## 4. Pet role card

The role card includes role name, one-sentence definition, three traits, memory point, content function, suitable scenes, unsuitable forced traits, camera/content/live fit, and long-term story potential.

The pet is never treated as a prop. Welfare, stable behavior, and realistic appearance time constrain production and live plans.

## 5. Audience hierarchy

- `CORE`: easiest to serve, trust, and monetize sustainably.
- `EXTENDED`: reachable through broader topics without changing the account's core promise.
- `EXCLUDED`: audiences the account should not repeatedly chase for vanity traffic.

Segments must include a concrete ownership stage, scenario, pain point, decision barrier, and willingness to pay where relevant.

## 6. Content architecture

Every option includes four pillars whose ratios total 100%:

- traffic content attracts relevant reach;
- trust content proves judgment and experience;
- persona content makes the creator and relationship memorable;
- conversion content creates a specific purchase or action context.

Ratios change by account stage. Launch accounts generally need more traffic and trust; mature commerce accounts may increase conversion while preserving trust.

## 7. Three required options

### Stable

Prioritizes sustainability, low execution cost, stable labels, and a practical new-account start.

### Differentiated

Prioritizes personal experience, relationship contrast, competitive separation, and memory.

### Commerce-oriented

Prioritizes high-value users, product fit, live conversion, service capacity, and fulfillment reality.

Each option contains positioning, creator persona, pet persona, relationship, audience, value, pillars, platform strategy, monetization, live fit, execution difficulty, advantages, disadvantages, risks, and recommendation score.

## 8. Selection and approval

Employees may select one option or create a mixed draft. Mixing must record which components came from which option and why. The formal report remains `DRAFT` until reviewed and explicitly approved by a project lead.

## 9. Recalibration

Content and live results feed back into evidence, not directly into approved positioning. A material positioning change starts a new session and report version so the company can distinguish learning from silent drift.
